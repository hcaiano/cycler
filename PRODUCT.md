# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Cycler is for macOS keyboard-first users who keep several app windows open and want fast,
predictable app/window navigation without using the Dock, Mission Control, or the mouse.

## Product Purpose

Cycler binds shortcuts to apps. A single-app shortcut jumps to the app and then cycles that
app's windows. A multi-app shortcut groups related apps under one key and cycles between the
apps in the configured order.

Success means the shortcut behavior is easy to predict, fast to build into muscle memory, and
configurable from a native macOS settings window.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, precise, utilitarian. Cycler should feel like a small native Mac utility: clear,
trustworthy, and out of the way.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing-style settings, decorative UI, clever but hidden behavior, nonstandard controls,
and configuration that feels accidental. App groups must look intentional, not like duplicate
shortcut rows that happened to share a key.

## Design Principles

- Preserve the simple mental model: one shortcut row has one clear behavior.
- Prefer native macOS controls and familiar Settings patterns over bespoke UI.
- Keep configuration compact, readable, and immediately applied.
- Make order visible when order affects behavior.
- Keep advanced behavior explicit instead of hidden behind state-dependent shortcuts.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use system fonts, system control states, clear focusable controls, SF Symbols where appropriate,
and labels/help text for icon-only actions. Respect reduced-motion expectations by avoiding
decorative motion in Settings.
