Jump to an app. Press again to walk its windows.

Bind a hotkey to an app once. The first press brings it to the front; press the same key again to step through that app's open windows, one at a time, wrapping around. No Dock hunting, no Mission Control. It lives in your menu bar and stays out of the way.

One key per app

Pick an app and record a shortcut: Hyper + 1 for Chrome, Hyper + 2 for your editor. The first press always takes you straight there, no matter what's in front.

⌃⌥⇧⌘1 ⌃⌥⇧⌘2 ⌃⌥⇧⌘3

Walk every window, not just two

Press the same key again and Cycler steps to that app's next window, then the next, and wraps back to the first. It keeps a stable order, so a dozen windows stay reachable. You never get stuck flipping between the same two.

↻

See what you landed on

A small, unobtrusive HUD shows the app icon, the window's title, and where you are in the cycle (2 / 4), then fades away. It never steals focus, so your typing goes exactly where you expect.

Reverse, and launch on demand

Add Shift to step backward through the windows. And if a bound app isn't running yet, the first press just launches it, so the same key always means "take me to this app."

⇧ + ↺

Up and running in a minute

  1. Download the app and drag it into your Applications folder.
  2. Allow it once in System Settings under Accessibility, so Cycler can raise your windows.
  3. Bind your keys from the menu bar: choose an app, record a shortcut, and start jumping.
