Jump to an app. Press again to walk its windows.

Bind a hotkey to an app. Press it to jump there; press it again to step through that app's windows. It lives in the menu bar, no Dock or Mission Control needed.

Download for Mac View on GitHub

Requires macOS 13 or later.

One key per app

Give each app a shortcut: Hyper + 1 for Chrome, Hyper + 2 for your editor. Press it and you're there, whatever was in front.

⌃⌥⇧⌘1 ⌃⌥⇧⌘2 ⌃⌥⇧⌘3

Cycle its windows

Press the same key again to go to the next window, then the next, wrapping back to the first.

↻

See where you are

A small overlay shows the window's title and your spot in the cycle (2 / 4), then disappears. It never takes focus.

Downloads 3 / 4

Reverse and launch

Hold Shift to step backward. If the app isn't open yet, the first press launches it.

⇧ + ↺

Setup

  1. Download Cycler and drag it into Applications.
  2. Allow it once under System Settings → Accessibility, so it can raise your windows.
  3. Bind your keys from the menu bar: pick an app, record a shortcut.
